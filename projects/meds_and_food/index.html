Why Vigilance Matters: The Safety Gap

In the past, food was simpler and medicines rare. Today, the Safety Gap emerges: people take multiple medications while consuming processed foods and nutrient-altered produce (due to soil depletion). Unknown interactions thrive in this gap ‚Äî turning "healthy" choices dangerous.

Polypharmacy Era: As prescriptions rise (37‚Äì45% of older adults on 5+ meds), food-medicine conflicts become near-certain without cross-checking. Blind consumption is no longer viable.

Hidden Interactions ‚Äì Real Example

Warfarin + Spinach: A "healthy" person on warfarin (blood thinner) must be vigilant about spinach (high in vitamin K). Sudden large intake can counteract warfarin's effect ‚Üí raised clot risk. Consistent low-moderate intake is safer ‚Äî but sudden changes require INR monitoring and possible dose adjustment. This proves even nutrient-rich foods aren't universally safe in the polypharmacy era.
